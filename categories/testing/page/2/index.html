By Tarek Dief in Testing, Embedded, Autonomous Flight

June 17, 2019

Project Overview: Airborne Wind Energy System (AWES) Step 1. Understand the Objective The Airborne Wind Energy System (AWES) aims to generate power using fixed-wing drones that utilize wind energy efficiently. The system features a rigid-body drone that flies in a Figure-of-Eight pattern, controlled by algorithms to harvest optimal power from the lift generated by its wings. Step 2. Learn the Key Details This innovative approach, demonstrated in this video, involves the use of drones to capture wind energy, offering a promising alternative to traditional wind turbines with potentially higher efficiency and lower environmental impact.

Project Overview: Quad-Rotor VTOL Drone Control System Step 1. Understand the Objective The quad-rotor is a widely recognized VTOL (Vertical Take-Off and Landing) drone used in various applications, such as military surveillance and agricultural services, where high safety and low cost are essential. The challenge lies in developing a complex control strategy to manage uncertainties from wind and its mathematical model. Step 2. Learn the Key Details This project, as demonstrated in this video, focuses on creating advanced control mechanisms for a quad-rotor to operate effectively under uncertain conditions, such as varying wind forces and complex dynamics, while maintaining low operational costs and high safety standards.
