Quad-rotor

By Tarek Dief August 3, 2014

Project Overview: Quad-Rotor VTOL Drone Control System

Step 1. Understand the Objective

The quad-rotor is a widely recognized VTOL (Vertical Take-Off and Landing) drone used in various applications, such as military surveillance and agricultural services, where high safety and low cost are essential. The challenge lies in developing a complex control strategy to manage uncertainties from wind and its mathematical model.

Step 2. Learn the Key Details

This project, as demonstrated in this video, focuses on creating advanced control mechanisms for a quad-rotor to operate effectively under uncertain conditions, such as varying wind forces and complex dynamics, while maintaining low operational costs and high safety standards.

Step 3. Know the Ultimate Goal

The ultimate goal is to develop a robust control system for the quad-rotor drone that ensures stability and reliability in various operational environments, enabling it to perform efficiently in applications that require VTOL capabilities.

Step 4. Understand My Responsibilities

  1. Non-Linear Mathematical Modeling: Develop a non-linear mathematical model to accurately represent the drone’s dynamics under various conditions.
  2. Control Strategy Design: Design and implement autonomous control strategies using PID (Proportional-Integral-Derivative) and fuzzy logic to handle uncertainties and optimize performance.
  3. Adaptive Flight-Path Control: Create adaptive flight-path control algorithms that adjust dynamically based on environmental conditions and mission requirements.
  4. System Development: Build the entire quad-rotor system from scratch, integrating all components to ensure seamless operation and performance.
